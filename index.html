Daily Coherence Pulse
Seven signals. One minute. No judgment.
This phone-friendly check-in tracks the conditions of coherence over time: energy, horizon, connection, clarity, identity, recovery, and Beauty/Awe/Meaning.
The pattern matters more than any single score. Low coherence is not failure. It is information about pressure, support, recovery, and the next small agency-preserving move. Beauty/Awe is included as a core signal because meaning, wonder, sacredness, humor, art, nature, and gratitude can help a person stay coherent when the pressure field narrows.
A relational question to hold

Is there anyone whose capacity to act from their values is being shaped — supported or limited — by your situation, decisions, or context right now?

You do not need to solve this. Just notice it.

Privacy: Daily Pulse readings are stored locally and included in JSON export/import. They are sent to the backend only when you ask the Twin to interpret them.
Safety: The Daily Pulse is a personal coherence reflection log. It is not a medical, psychological, or mental-health diagnostic tool.

Seven Signals
These are not medical vital signs. They are coherence signals for self-navigation.
  • Physical Energy: capacity available today.
  • Temporal Horizon: ability to see beyond immediate pressure.
  • Relational Coherence: connection and BioBond support.
  • Structural Clarity: whether the field makes sense.
  • Identity Integrity: feeling like oneself under pressure.
  • Recovery Access: room to rest, repair, or reset.
  • Beauty / Awe / Meaning: contact with what is beautiful, sacred, inspiring, humorous, creative, or larger than the pressure field.
The Daily Pulse supports the Twin; it does not replace the Twin conversation. The user chooses what, if anything, becomes part of long-term Twin memory.

Navigator Field Scan
Map the system you are inside: what it rewards, what it punishes, where your effort routes, where agency is suppressed, and what the next agency-preserving move might be.
What is this?

A Field Scan is a situational map. It helps the Twin understand the environments acting on you: school, work, family, research, finances, community, or any field where agency may route or get suppressed.

Your Field Map

Your Twin learns who you are from conversation, what you create from Document Capsules, and what is acting on you from Field Scans. Several scans help reveal the combined situation-context in which action becomes possible, costly, urgent, meaningful, or blocked.

Moves Workspace
Generate Move Cards from saved Field Scans, then compare possible moves across fields without letting the app decide for you.
Move comparison is a map, not a command. Use this workspace after you have at least two saved Move Cards. The decision lenses help you notice different kinds of reasons: urgency, future agency, suppression relief, support readiness, and low-risk first steps. You remain the chooser.
How to use Moves without getting lost
Start with one Field Scan and generate a few Move Cards. Save only the cards that feel plausible. When you have moves from more than one field, compare two to four at a time, set one Current Move, then ask the Twin to deliberate with you.
  • Move Cards show possible agency-preserving actions inside one field.
  • Cross-Field Comparison helps you see which saved moves may matter now across your larger context.
  • Current Move means “the move I am presently considering,” not “the app has chosen for me.”
Current Move Deliberation

Turn “I might do this” into “here is the smallest coherent first step.” This plan does not decide for you. It helps you slow the move down, name what it preserves, identify support, and set a check-back point before acting.

Coach Design Rules
These rules keep the Coaches useful and safe.
  • Minimal context: use only the local memory needed for the task.
  • No silent authority: a Coach may suggest distinctions, not decide for the user.
  • PRF humility: the PRF profile is a starting map, never a verdict.
  • OS1/OS2 discipline: distinguish shared meaning from shared coordination.
  • Reasoning literacy: deduction, induction, abduction, analogy, and fallacy detection are treated as tools, each with limits.

What Navigator does

Navigator helps a user talk with a Digital Twin, preserve memory through Journal entries and Document Capsules, map life-fields through Field Scans, assemble the combined context, generate possible Move Cards, compare moves across fields, and create a small Current Move Plan for deliberation.

What Navigator does not do

  • It does not diagnose, treat, grade, command, or replace professional advice.
  • It does not decide who the user is or what the user must do.
  • It does not store raw uploaded documents after a Document Capsule is created.
  • It does not cache backend AI calls, API responses, or raw uploaded document text.

Privacy and security posture

Local-first by design: conversation, Journal entries, Daily Pulse readings, PRF-22 profile summaries, Document Capsules, Field Scans, Context Assembly, Move Cards, comparison state, Current Move Plans, and Coach Notes are stored in this browser unless the user exports or restores them.

Backend transparency: backend AI calls occur only when the user asks the Twin to interpret, summarize, deliberate, create a capsule, or generate an AI-assisted response. Those requests require sending selected prompt context to the configured backend service for processing. PRF-22 import stores the raw JSON locally and sends only a short profile summary when Twin Context is turned on. Users should avoid uploading highly sensitive material unless they understand that selected content may be sent for AI processing.

User control: users can export JSON, export TXT, import/restore JSON, delete saved items, and clear local memory. Document uploads create reviewable capsules; the app saves only the capsule the user approves, not the raw uploaded document text.

Sample / demo data mode

Use the fictional demo to show the full workflow quickly. It creates a sample Twin conversation, Journal, PRF-22 profile, Document Capsule, three Field Scans, Context Assembly, Move Cards, a Current Move, and a Current Move Plan. It will replace local memory only after confirmation.

PWA behavior and safety checklist

  • This PWA caches only the app shell: index, manifest, service worker, and icons.
  • Do not cache backend AI calls or API responses.
  • Do not cache raw uploaded document text.
  • Keep JSON export/import visible and working before and after PWA conversion.
  • The service worker is versioned. When a new version is deployed, reload after activation if the browser prompts or shows stale behavior.
  • Test phone and laptop layouts separately: phone for capture/continuity, laptop for deep review and planning.
  • Keep optional encrypted sync as a later goal, not part of this stable local-first build.
Help / Tutorial
A quick guide to using Navigator without needing the theory first.

Quick Start

  1. Click Start / Resume Twin.
  2. Reply briefly and honestly. The Twin should ask one question at a time.
  3. After a few replies, expand the Twin Journal to see what the Twin has cautiously learned.
  4. Upload a document if you want the Twin to understand a project or research style.
  5. Run a Field Scan if you want to map an environment such as school, work, family, finances, or research.
  6. Generate Move Cards from saved Field Scans, then use Cross-Field Move Comparison to compare two to four possible moves.
  7. Set one Current Move when you are ready to deliberate. Current does not mean final; it means “the move I am now considering.”
  8. Create a Current Move Plan that names the smallest first action, support needed, risk to watch, non-sacrifice point, and check-back moment.
  9. Export JSON before changing browsers, devices, or clearing memory.

Three ways to use Navigator

  • Self-understanding: talk with the Twin and review the Twin Journal.
  • Research or creative work: upload documents and build capsules that show themes, style, and open questions.
  • Life navigation: run Field Scans to understand which environments support or suppress agency.
  • Action deliberation: compare saved Move Cards across fields before choosing what to treat as the current next move.

Example use case

A student feels stuck in a course. They talk with the Twin and notice they care about meaningful work, not just grades. They run a Field Scan and see that effort feels absorbed by compliance tasks. They identify one BioBond: a study partner who helps them think clearly. Their next move is not perfect, but it preserves agency.
